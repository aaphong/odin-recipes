What You Need To Make the BEST Beef Raviolis:

The Sauce
  • 3 roma tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 3/4 cup greek yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon chopped fresh mint leaves
  • 1/8 red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons water
The Meat
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup dried yellow split peas
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 cube chicken bouillon
The Pasta
  • 26 wonton wrappers
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt

Putting It All Together!

  1. In a small bowl, stir together yogurt, mint, and garlic. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. In a large skillet over medium heat, cook ground beef and onions until the meat is browned, stirring occasionally to crumble; drain fat. Stir in cup water, carrot, salt, black pepper, coriander, and cumin; simmer uncovered for 30 minutes or until water evaporates.
  3. Lay out wonton wrappers on aluminum foil. Mound two large spoonfuls of beef mixture in the center of one wrapper. Dip finger in water and trace it around all four edges. Lay a second wonton wrapper on top of the first, and press on all edges to seal. Roll up the edges to create a round, hat-shaped ravioli. Repeat, making 13 ravioli.
  4. Place ravioli in steamer and steam for 40 minutes.
  5. Meanwhile, mix together the yellow split peas, pepper flakes, coriander, cumin, chicken boullion, and water in a saucepan. Bring to a boil, then simmer over medium-low heat for about 45 minutes, or until it reaches a thick slurry-like consistency.
  6. Meanwhile, in a large skillet, mix remaining beef mixture with water, tomato paste, and pepper flakes. Simmer over low heat until liquid has evaporated.
  7. To serve, spread yogurt on a serving plate. Top with ravioli, and top again with split pea mixture. Finally, top with tomato and beef mixture. Serve immediately and enjoy!
